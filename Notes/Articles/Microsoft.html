My Projects at Microsoft

Introduction

Last Summer I was fortunate enough to be a Summer intern at Microsoft. (Now, that in itself was an experience, and I urge you to take a look at my article detailing my experiences). The Summer was particularily interesting because I had the chance to develop two great apps that helped us get a better understanding of the Highlights and Pain Points facing developers who wish to develop on the new Universal Application Development Environment.

In this article, I wanted to give you a quick idea of what the two applications did, so as to provide you with a better understanding of the sorts of work I did this last Summer.

Open States

I developed the first application in order to get a better understanding of how difficult it would be to port an application from iOS to Windows. As such, I chose the Open States iOS application.

The concept behind OpenStates was simple enough. The application pulls data from Sun Light Labs, Open States API. The data regarding Politicians, their constiuencies, and their policies, are then displayed to the user in the iOS app.

For my part, I developed a Universal Application using the C# Programming Language and the Universal Application Framework. The final product was then demonstrated to my team, and I wrote a report detailing my experiences. Unfortunately, the information contained in that report is not available for the public.

Recipe Guide

The second application was developed in order to test Apache Cordova. This absolutely wonderful tool allows developers to write one single application in HTML/CSS and Javascript , and then deploy that app on a wide variety of mobile and tablet form factors.

Recipe Guide, the application I built, simply got information from Yummly and displayed the information to the user. The actual application allowed users to have profiles, store favourite recipes, create grocery lists from recipies, and view the nutritional and taste information. I wrote the application using HTML/CSS and Javascript and deployed it on a variety of form factors, including multiple Windows phone devices, an iPhone, and an Android Tablet.

Looking Forward

I am pleased to be returning back to Microsoft in the Fall to continue my work on improving the developer experience. I will be working with the Universal Applications Team as a Software Development Engineer in Seattle, WA.
